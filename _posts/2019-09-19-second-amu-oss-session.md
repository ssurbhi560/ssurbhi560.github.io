Today we had a session taken by Husain bhai who just completed his 2 months internship at Microsoft, Hyderabad as Software Engineer and also got a full-time offer to join Microsoft after.

So, even before the session started, the room was already full, no seats were left, so you can imagine what crowd must be there.

He started the session by sharing his experience that how he learned game development in school, then after getting into college, joined roboclub and learned more from his seniors. 
In summer vacations of first year he developed different skills by learning things like web development. Further he shared that how these skills (web development, android development etc.) are important, not only to take part in competitions and hackathons, but these also helps you to think of various creative ideas on which, you can then implement these skills. 
He moved further by answering that "What is a hackathon?". It's like a startup in a day (or whatever is time limit of the hackathon) which you have to sell to judges by presenting your idea to them.  He also mentioned the importance of projects, and that how doing so many projects helped him in his interview for Microsoft internship. 

I also learned a few things in first year but never did any projects on them, so now I am aiming to start working on a few projects, this will not only add to my profile but will also help me to learn more while working on them. 
He also laid emphasis on working on your soft skills- interacting more with smart people, learning from them, and how not to feel that just because of your college or city you live in that you don't have enough opportunities! We have got people around us who have great knowledge in so many different fields, learn from them. Opportunities are always there, you just need to look for them properly.
And here is one tip from him, "if you want to learn software engineering, learn tools-and the best way to do this is by contributing to open source". So, here comes the Open source again, a place where you can learn so much so that no class no university can teach you. From communicating to different technical skills, it teaches you alot of things.
I'm also looking forward to contribute to open source soon after learning to do so from sessions organized by AMU-OSS. 

After that he also suggested a path for juniors, that they can follow :-

1.In first year, learn and explore as much as you can. **Develop the skills.** 

2.In second year **compete**. Take part in hackathons and other competitions, do research work and build as many projects as you can. 

3.In third year, apply for technical or research  internships. This is the most important year to polish your CV by doing internships. 

4.In fourth year just prepare for placements or masters, whatever you planning to do after graduation. 

Currently, I'm learning, and trying to build some projects, also working on a research paper. Hoping to complete them soon. 
One more thing I'd like to mention, that he told was that- you can just learn from everyone, your seniors, juniors, teachers and your friends too. You just need to surround yourself with those people and start asking questions . Learn things other than that are in your course syllabus, and always have a high self-esteem. 

At last he just asked everyone to "JUST START" take the first step, it's the hardest but take it anyway, because you don't have any excuse to not to do that, and at last Husain bhai said **"Action is always better than inaction"**.
