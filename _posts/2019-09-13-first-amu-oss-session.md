The first session of AMU-OSS after orientation was held on 11 September 2019, Wednesday.
In the session Rahul sir shared his experience and journey to GSoC (Google summer of code) 2017. He told everyone the practices that he followed, which helped him alot and some of those are:
1.Using Linux over Windows. 
2.Using [duckduckgo](https://duckduckgo.com) as your search engine instead  of Google Chrome. 
3.Asking questions properly. That is only after searching for the solution for your problem thoroughly on internet on your own. A guide for this, which one can refer to is [here](http://www.catb.org/~esr/faqs/smart-questions.html) 

Apart form these things he also shared with everyone how his journey for GSoC started with an email that he sent to one of the PhD student in Switzerland, asking him about all the things that he wanted to know regarding the program. He also told students that technical skills are important but other than that one more skill that helps you the most in achieving anything is having good communication skills.

At last he advised the students to look for some open source organisation of their interest and atleast just try reading their beginners guide and also asked the students to join AMU-OSS slack workspace for any further questions or queries.
